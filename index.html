Digital Security Guide

Learn how to protect yourself on the internet in a simple way

Hello! The internet is a wonderful place to chat and learn, but we need to take some precautions. Check out the important tips below:

1. Watch Out for Links on WhatsApp

If you received a message from a bank, a famous brand, or even an acquaintance saying you won a prize or need to update your data urgently, do not click on the link.

Real banks do not ask for passwords or urgent data via message.

2. Create Secure Passwords

Avoid using your birth date, grandchildren's names, or simple sequences like "123456".

A good tip is to use a phrase you like and mix it with numbers. Example: CoffeeWithCake2026. Write it down in a notebook at home so you don't forget.

3. When in Doubt, Ask for Help

If something seems strange, too good to be true, or if you are afraid of doing an operation on your phone, stop and ask for help from a child, grandchild, or someone you fully trust.

It's better to ask twice than to fall into a trap.

Questions? Call a family member or talk to your bank.
